📌 Why This Chapter Matters
📊

Risk Decomposition

Total risk = Systematic + Unsystematic. Only systematic risk is priced by the market — this is the foundation of modern finance.

🔗

Diversification Power

Combining assets with low correlation reduces portfolio risk below the weighted average of individual risks. Correlation is the key variable.

📈

CAPM in Practice

CAPM links β (systematic risk) to required return. Used in investment appraisal, valuation, and regulatory rate-setting worldwide.

📚 Module Breakdown
1

Expected Return & Variance 期望收益与方差

E(R) = Σ pᵢ × Rᵢ; Variance σ² = Σ pᵢ(Rᵢ − E(R))². The building blocks of all portfolio math.

CalculatorExam Core
2

Covariance & Correlation 协方差与相关系数

Cov(A,B) = Σ pᵢ(Rₐ−E(Rₐ))(R_b−E(R_b)); ρ = Cov/σₐσ_b. Correlation ranges from −1 to +1.

CalculatorExam Core
3

Two-Asset Portfolio Risk 两资产组合风险

σ²_p = w²_A σ²_A + w²_B σ²_B + 2w_A w_B Cov(A,B). Adjust weights to see risk change.

CalculatorExam Core
4

Efficient Frontier & Diversification 有效前沿

The set of portfolios offering maximum return for a given risk level. Rational investors choose on the frontier.

ConceptualExam Core
5

CAPM & Beta CAPM与贝塔系数

K_e = R_f + β(R_m − R_f). Beta measures sensitivity to market movements. β=1 moves with market; β>1 amplifies; β<1 dampens.

🔑 Key Formulas at a Glance
E(R) = Σ pᵢ × Rᵢ σ² = Σ pᵢ × (Rᵢ − E(R))² σ²_p = w²_A σ²_A + w²_B σ²_B + 2·w_A·w_B·Cov(A,B) Cov(A,B) = Σ pᵢ(Rₐ−E(Rₐ))(R_b−E(R_b)) K_e = R_f + β(R_m − R_f) ← CAPM
⚠️ Exam Pitfall Rapid-Fire
❌ Trap 1: "Portfolio standard deviation = weighted average of individual σ's"
→ FALSE. You must include the covariance term. σ_p is always ≤ weighted average (when ρ < 1).
❌ Trap 2: "Diversification eliminates all risk"
→ FALSE. Only specific (unsystematic) risk is eliminated. Systematic risk remains no matter how many assets you hold.
❌ Trap 3: "A high standard deviation means a high required return (CAPM)"
→ FALSE. CAPM uses beta (systematic risk only), not total σ. A high-σ stock with low beta may have a lower required return than a low-σ stock with high beta.
❌ Trap 4: "Diversification is only beneficial when correlation is negative"
→ FALSE. Any ρ < +1 gives some diversification benefit. You don't need negative correlation — just less than perfect positive correlation.
