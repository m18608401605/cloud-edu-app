Section B Style Question 1

Practice Question — Based on Lecture 3 examples | Marks: 20

🔢 Calculation · Incremental Cash Flow + Tax Shield

Greenfield Manufacturing plc is evaluating a new production line. The following information is available:

ItemAmount
Initial investment in machinery£500,000
Project life5 years
Annual incremental revenue£300,000
Annual incremental operating costs (excl. depreciation)£120,000
Depreciation methodStraight-line over 5 years, zero residual
Corporation tax rate25%
Cost of capital10%
Tax paid in same year as profit earnedYes
Sunk cost: feasibility study already paid£30,000
Opportunity cost: warehouse rental foregone p.a.£20,000

Required:

  1. Calculate the annual incremental free cash flow. (8 marks)
  2. Calculate the NPV of the project. (8 marks)
  3. Should the company proceed? Justify your answer. (4 marks)
✅ Full Solution

Note: Sunk cost (£30,000 feasibility study) is EXCLUDED — already spent, irrelevant to decision. Opportunity cost (£20,000 warehouse) is INCLUDED — real foregone benefit.

Step 1: Annual Depreciation

£500,000 ÷ 5 years= £100,000 p.a.

Step 2: Annual Incremental Free Cash Flow

Incremental Revenue£300,000
Less: Operating Costs−£120,000
Less: Opportunity Cost (warehouse)−£20,000
Less: Depreciation−£100,000
EBIT£60,000
Less: Tax at 25%−£15,000
Earnings after tax£45,000
Add back: Depreciation+£100,000
Annual Free Cash Flow£145,000

Step 3: NPV Calculation (Annuity factor for 5 years at 10% = 3.791)

PV of annual cash flows: £145,000 × 3.791£549,695
Less: Initial Investment−£500,000
NPV+£49,695

Decision: NPV is positive (+£49,695), therefore the company should PROCEED with the project. The investment creates value for shareholders. Note: the £30,000 sunk cost is irrelevant to this decision.

Section B Style Question 2

Practice Question — Capital Rationing with Profitability Index | Marks: 15

🔢 Calculation · Capital Rationing · Profitability Index

Apex Capital plc has £600,000 available for investment this year. Three mutually independent projects are available:

ProjectInitial InvestmentNPV
Alpha£400,000£80,000
Beta£300,000£75,000
Gamma£200,000£60,000

Projects are divisible (you can invest a fraction of each project).

Required:

  1. Calculate the Profitability Index for each project. (6 marks)
  2. Determine the optimal investment plan and total NPV achievable. (9 marks)
✅ Full Solution

Step 1: Profitability Index = NPV ÷ Initial Investment

Alpha: £80,000 ÷ £400,000PI = 0.20
Beta: £75,000 ÷ £300,000PI = 0.25
Gamma: £60,000 ÷ £200,000PI = 0.30

Step 2: Rank by PI (highest first): Gamma → Beta → Alpha

Step 3: Optimal Allocation (£600,000 budget)

Invest fully in Gamma (PI=0.30): £200,000NPV: £60,000
Invest fully in Beta (PI=0.25): £300,000NPV: £75,000
Remaining budget: £600,000 − £500,000 = £100,000
Invest £100,000 in Alpha (25% of £400,000): NPV = 25% × £80,000NPV: £20,000
Total NPV£155,000

Key insight: Ranking by absolute NPV would give Alpha first (£80,000), but that leaves only £200,000 for Gamma — total NPV = £80,000 + £60,000 = £140,000. The PI method gives £155,000 — £15,000 more value from the same budget.
