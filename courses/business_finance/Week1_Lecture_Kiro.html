📘 Chapter 1 — The Nature of Business Finance

📚 Core Concept: What is a Limited Company? 有限公司是什么？

费曼类比 Feynman Analogy

把有限公司想象成一个独立的机器人。这个机器人可以签合同、拥有财产、被起诉——但它不是任何一个人。股东只是"给机器人充电的人"，机器人欠的债，不会追到充电人身上。

A limited company is a separate legal entity — it can own assets, enter contracts, and be sued independently of its shareholders. Shareholders enjoy limited liability: their maximum loss is what they invested.

01

Separate Legal Entity

The company exists independently of its owners. It can sue and be sued in its own name.

02

Limited Liability

Shareholders can only lose what they paid for their shares — personal assets are protected.

03

Perpetual Succession

The company continues to exist even if shareholders change or die.

⚠️ 常见陷阱 Exam Pitfall

Many students confuse "limited liability" with the company having a right to limit its debts. Wrong!

✅ Rule: It is the shareholders who have limited liability — not the company. The company itself is fully liable for ALL its debts.

In terms of business activity volume, limited companies dominate the UK economy — not partnerships or co-operatives.

⚠️ 陷阱 Pitfall

Q: "Who has most to gain if the business does well AND most to lose if it does badly?"

✅ Answer: Ordinary shareholders — they are the residual claimants. Debenture holders get fixed returns regardless.

📚 Risk & Return Relationship 风险与回报

核心原则

Higher risk → Higher required return. But the relationship is not proportional.

If Investment X is twice as risky as Y, the required return from X is larger than Y's, but less than twice as large. Why? Because there is a risk-free rate of return (e.g., government bonds) that applies to both — only the risk premium doubles, not the total return.

🏦 Real World Example

UK Government Bonds vs. Tech Startup Equity

If UK gilts yield 4% (risk-free), and a startup equity requires a 10% risk premium, the total required return is 14%. If another startup is "twice as risky", its risk premium doubles to 20%, giving a total of 24% — not 28% (which would be twice 14%).

📗 Chapter 2 — Financial Objectives & the Separation Theorem

📚 The Corporate Objective 企业目标

核心答案

The generally accepted corporate objective in business finance is: Maximisation of Shareholders' Wealth — NOT profit maximisation.

Why NOT profit maximisation? 为什么不是利润最大化？

Problem with Profit MaxWhy it Fails
Ignores timing£1 today ≠ £1 in 10 years (time value of money)
Ignores riskA risky £100 profit ≠ a certain £100 profit
Ignores long-termCutting R&D boosts this year's profit but destroys future value
Ignores wealth generationProfit ≠ cash flow; accounting profit can be manipulated
💥 Case Study — Bed Bath & Beyond

The Danger of Short-Term Profit Focus

Bed Bath & Beyond spent ~$11.8 billion on stock buybacks since 2004, boosting short-term EPS (earnings per share) and appearing profitable. But this depleted cash reserves. When the macro environment deteriorated, they couldn't service their debts — and filed for bankruptcy in 2023. Short-term profit maximisation destroyed long-term shareholder wealth.

📚 Agency Problem & Agency Costs 代理问题

费曼类比

你雇了一个管家（经理）来管理你的房子（公司）。但管家可能会用你的钱买他自己喜欢的家具。Agency costs = 你为了监督管家、防止他乱花钱而付出的代价。

Agency costs = the cost to shareholders of managers NOT acting in shareholders' best interests (monitoring costs, bonding costs, residual loss).

⚠️ 陷阱

Agency costs are NOT advertising costs, travel costs, or costs of hiring temp staff.

✅ Agency costs = costs arising from the principal-agent conflict between shareholders and managers.

📚 The Separation Theorem 分离定理

核心思想

The separation theorem says: investment decisions and consumption decisions can be made separately — because shareholders can borrow/lend at the market rate to adjust their personal consumption timing.

Key Assumptions 关键假设

  • Returns from investments are known with certainty
  • Individuals can borrow and lend at the same market interest rate
  • No taxes or transaction costs

Key Implications 关键推论

→

Investment Decision

Business should invest in all positive-NPV projects — regardless of shareholders' personal consumption preferences.

→

Dividend Irrelevance

Shareholders don't necessarily care how much dividend they receive — they can borrow/lend to adjust their own cash flow timing.

→

Financing Irrelevance

Shareholders' wealth is NOT affected by the financing method used (debt vs equity) — under the theorem's assumptions.

⚠️ 高频陷阱 High-Frequency Pitfall

The separation theorem says investment and consumption should be separated — NOT investment and financing.

✅ The two areas: Investment decisions vs. Consumption decisions (not financing!)
📊 Exam Classic Question

Q: The separation theorem implies that shareholders would:

  • A. Always prefer maximum dividend
  • B. Always prefer 50% dividend
  • C. Always prefer no dividend
  • D. Not necessarily care how much dividend they receive ✅

Because they can borrow or lend at the market rate to achieve their preferred consumption pattern regardless of dividend policy.
