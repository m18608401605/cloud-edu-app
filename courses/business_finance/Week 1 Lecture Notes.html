Lecture 1: Introduction to Business Finance

第一讲：商业金融导论 (详尽讲义)

1. What is Business Finance?

1. 什么是商业金融？

1.1 General Financial Concepts

1.1 基本金融概念

Economics vs. Finance

经济学与金融学的区别

  • Economics: The science of how scarce resources—natural resources, land, labor, capital—are allocated.
  • Finance: The sub-field focused specifically on the allocation of capital.
  • Core Function: Finance funnels capital (money) from those with a surplus of money and a deficit of ideas to those with a deficit of money and a surplus of ideas.
  • 经济学 (Economics): 研究稀缺资源（自然资源、土地、劳动力、资本）如何分配的科学。
  • 金融学 (Finance): 专注于“资本分配”这一特定领域的经济学分支。
  • 核心功能: 金融将资本（金钱）从“有闲钱但缺想法”的人手中，输送给“有想法但缺资金”的人。

1.2 Business and Business Finance

1.2 商业与商业金融的核心

The Core Questions

两大核心问题

Business is ultimately about making money. Business finance is a distinct subject—mixing economics, accounting, law, quantitative methods, and behavioural sciences—that answers two fundamental questions:

商业的终极目的就是 赚钱 (Making money)。商业金融是一门融合了经济学、会计学、法律、定量分析和行为科学的独立学科，它主要解决两个根本问题：

  • 1. How to invest money? (Investment Decision / Capital Budgeting)
    • Whether to choose or not (e.g., investing in new technology, setting up a new production line, launching a new product).
    • Which specific project to choose among alternatives.
  • 2. How to raise money? (Financing Decision / Capital Structure)
    • Should the firm use Debt or Equity? (And how to issue them?)
    • How much debt and how much equity should make up the capital structure?
    • How to distribute profits? (Dividend policy)
  • 1. 如何投资？(投资决策 / 资本预算)
    • 是否进行某项投资（例如：投资新技术、建立新生产线、发布新产品）。
    • 在多个备选方案中，究竟应该选择哪一个。
  • 2. 如何筹资？(融资决策 / 资本结构)
    • 应该使用债务 (Debt) 还是股权 (Equity)？（以及如何发行？）
    • 企业的资本结构中应该包含多少债务和多少股权？
    • 赚到的利润应该如何分配？(股利政策)

2. Organization of Firms

2. 企业的组织形式

A business can be a sole proprietorship, partnership, or a limited company/corporation. This course focuses heavily on limited companies, although the theories apply universally.

企业可以是独资企业、合伙企业或有限责任公司/股份有限公司。虽然我们将学习的财务理论适用于所有企业，但本课程的重点是有限责任公司。

2.1 Traditional Legal Structures

2.1 传统法律结构

Sole Proprietorship (Default Legal Structure)

独资企业 (默认法定结构)

  • Managed by 1 owner/manager.
  • Limited to the life of the owner (dissolves upon death).
  • Unlimited liability: The owner is personally responsible for all business debts.
  • Taxation occurs at the individual level (no corporate tax).
  • 由 1 位所有者/管理者经营。
  • 寿命仅限于所有者的寿命（所有者死亡即解散）。
  • 无限责任 (Unlimited liability): 所有者对企业的所有债务承担无限的个人连带责任。
  • 在个人层面征税（无需缴纳企业所得税）。

Partnerships (General and Limited)

合伙企业 (普通合伙与有限合伙)

  • Involves multiple owners/managers.
  • The partnership dissolves when one partner dies or wishes to sell. It is difficult to transfer ownership.
  • Taxation is at the individual level.
  • General Partnerships: Each partner is fully (unlimitedly) responsible for all liabilities incurred by the partnership.
  • Limited Partnerships: The 'limited owners' have limited liability (capped at their investment amount). Their death/withdrawal does not dissolve the firm, and their interest is transferable. However, they have no management authority.
  • 涉及多个所有者/管理者。
  • 当一名合伙人死亡或希望出售股份时，合伙企业即告解散。所有权极难转移。
  • 在个人层面征税。
  • 普通合伙 (General Partnerships): 每位合伙人对合伙企业产生的所有债务承担完全的无限责任。
  • 有限合伙 (Limited Partnerships): “有限合伙人”享有有限责任（仅限于其投资额）。他们的死亡或退出不会解散企业，且其权益可以转让。但是，他们没有企业管理权。

2.2 Limited Company / Corporation

2.2 有限责任公司 / 股份有限公司

Variations globally include U.S.: Inc, UK: Ltd or Plc, France: S.A., Italy: S.p.A., Sweden: AB.

全球的变体包括美国：Inc，英国：Ltd 或 Plc，法国：S.A.，意大利：S.p.A.，瑞典：AB。

Benefits

优势 (Benefits)

  • Capital Generation: The ability to raise massive amounts of capital is significantly better.
  • Limited Liability & Unlimited Life: Owners (shareholders) are protected by limited liability, and the corporation has an unlimited lifespan.
  • Delegation: No special skills are needed to be a shareholder, as shareholders delegate managerial responsibilities to professionals.
  • Liquidity: Ownership is divisible, allowing investors to diversify portfolios, and shares can be readily transferred to new owners.
  • 强大的融资能力: 筹集大额资本的能力远超其他企业形式。
  • 有限责任与无限寿命: 所有者（股东）受有限责任保护（最多亏损买股票的钱），且公司具有无限的存续期。
  • 委托管理: 成为股东不需要任何特殊的商业技能，因为股东将管理责任委托给了职业经理人。
  • 流动性与分散风险: 所有权是可分割的（分成股票），允许投资者分散投资，并且股票可以随时转让给新所有者。

Costs and Vulnerabilities

代价与弱点 (Costs)

  • Double Taxation: Profits are taxed at the corporate level, and dividends are taxed again at the individual shareholder level.
  • Separation of Ownership and Control: This structural divide leads directly to the Principal-Agent conflict (The Agency Problem).
  • Asymmetric Information: Managers possess internal daily data that diffuse shareholders do not have. This requires disclosure laws and financial institutions to alleviate the problem.
  • 双重征税 (Double Taxation): 利润在公司层面先被征收企业所得税，分发给股东的股息在个人层面再次被征收个人所得税。
  • 所有权与控制权分离: 这种结构性的割裂直接导致了“委托-代理冲突”（即代理问题）。
  • 信息不对称 (Asymmetric Information): 管理层掌握着分散的股东所没有的内部日常数据。这必须依靠严格的信息披露法律和金融中介机构来缓解。

真实世界证据：极其分散的所有权 (Diffusion of Ownership)

为了说明为什么股东无法亲自进行日常管理，请看现实中公司的所有权是多么的分散和碎片化（数据来源：COMPUSTAT 2021）：

3. The Agency Problem & Corporate Governance

3. 代理问题与公司治理

3.1 The Agency Problem and Agency Costs

3.1 代理问题与代理成本

Conflict of Interests

利益冲突的核心

  • Shareholders' Best Interests: Long-term profitability and wealth maximization.
  • Managers' Best Interests: Often suffer from Short-Termism. They may prioritize their reputations while in the business, their personal remuneration (pay & perks), building corporate empires through unnecessary mergers and expansions, and reducing their employment risk through broad diversification.
  • 股东的最高利益: 长期盈利能力与财富最大化。
  • 管理层的最高利益: 往往患有 短视主义 (Short-Termism)。他们可能优先考虑自己在任期内的名声、个人的薪酬和奢华的在职消费 (perks)、通过不必要的并购扩张来建立“商业帝国 (Empire building)”，以及通过盲目的多元化来降低自己的失业风险。

Agency Costs

代理成本 (Agency Costs)

This principal-agent conflict results in additional costs to the business, known as Agency Costs. These include:

这种委托-代理冲突给企业带来了额外的成本，被称为 代理成本。这些成本包括：

  • The direct costs/losses to shareholders when managers make decisions that are not in the shareholders' best interests.
  • The expenditures incurred because shareholders need to monitor the managers (e.g., paying for independent external auditors).
  • 当管理者做出不符合股东最大利益的决策时，给股东造成的直接成本/损失。
  • 因为股东必须 监督 管理层而产生的支出（例如：花重金聘请独立的外部审计师进行审计）。

3.2 Corporate Governance

3.2 公司治理 (Corporate Governance)

The Role of Directors

董事会的角色

To mitigate these issues, rigorous rules exist to monitor and control the behaviour of directors. They are legally bound by the Duty of Care and the Duty of Loyalty. Governance is based on 3 guiding principles:

为了缓解上述问题，现代商业社会建立了严格的规则来监督和控制董事的行为。他们在法律上受制于 注意义务 (Duty of Care) 和 忠实义务 (Duty of Loyalty)。公司治理主要基于以下 3 个指导原则：

  • 1. Disclosure: Providing adequate and timely information about corporate performance to prevent information hiding.
  • 2. Accountability: Ensuring annual financial statements are independently audited.
  • 3. Fairness: Ensuring directors do not unfairly gain from insider knowledge (imposing strict restrictions on immediate buying and selling of company shares).
  • 1. 信息披露 (Disclosure): 充分、及时地提供有关企业绩效的信息，防止内部信息隐瞒。
  • 2. 问责制 (Accountability): 强制要求年度财务报表必须经过独立审计。
  • 3. 公平性 (Fairness): 确保董事不会利用内幕信息获取不当利益（对高管买卖本公司股票施加严格的限制）。

The Role of Shareholders & Recent Studies

股东的角色与最新研究观点

  • It is often argued that massive corporate failures happen because shareholders (especially large institutional investors) are not active enough in corporate governance. Shareholders have the power to actively compel a company to act correctly.
  • Recent Research nuance: While the US and UK exhibit widely dispersed ownership where the conflict is between managers and small shareholders, in East Asian and Continental European corporations, firms are closely held. In these regions, the primary conflict shifts: it becomes a conflict between controlling (majority) shareholders and minority shareholders.
  • 学术界通常认为，重大企业倒闭案件的发生，往往是因为股东（尤其是大型机构投资者）在公司治理中不够活跃。股东本有权力积极迫使公司纠正错误。
  • 最新研究的细微差别: 尽管美国和英国的企业所有权极为分散（主要矛盾是经理人与小股东的冲突），但在东亚和欧洲大陆的企业中，股权往往高度集中。在这些地区，主要矛盾发生了转移：变成了 控股（大）股东 与 少数（中小）股东 之间的利益冲突。

4. Business Objectives

4. 企业的商业目标

4.1 Maximisation of What?

4.1 究竟要最大化什么？

To evaluate whether the financial manager is making good decisions, we must define the ultimate goal. The lecture notes outline several potential objectives:

为了评估财务经理是否做出了好的决策，我们必须首先定义企业的终极目标。讲义列出了以下几个潜在目标：

  • Profit Maximization: A deeply flawed objective. It is highly susceptible to manipulation (Quantitative Difficulties), focuses heavily on the short-term (Timescale), and completely ignores the risk profile of the cash flows.
  • Long-term Stability & Survival: Essential, but defensive rather than wealth-creating.
  • Growth: Often pursued by managers (empire building) but does not automatically guarantee increased value for owners.
  • Satisficing: A behavioural concept where managers aim to achieve an "acceptable" or "satisfactory" level of return for shareholders while keeping other stakeholders (employees, suppliers) content, rather than pursuing absolute mathematical maximization.
  • Return of Capital: Focusing merely on getting the initial investment back.
  • 利润最大化 (Profit Maximization): 这是一个充满致命缺陷的目标。它极易被会计手段操纵（量化困难），严重聚焦于短期账面好看（时间短视），并且完全忽略了获取这些现金流所承担的巨大风险。
  • 长期稳定与生存 (Stability & Survival): 这是必须的，但这属于防御性目标，而非财富创造目标。
  • 企业增长 (Growth): 往往是管理层为了私利而追求的目标（建立商业帝国），但盲目扩大规模并不能自动保证所有者价值的增加。
  • 满意原则 (Satisficing): 这是一个行为经济学概念，指管理者只求为股东实现“可接受”或“令人满意”的回报水平，同时让其他利益相关者（员工、供应商等）过得去即可，而不是去追求绝对的、数学意义上的利益最大化。
  • 资本回报 (Return of Capital): 仅仅关注收回最初的投资成本。

Conclusion: Shareholder Wealth Maximization

最终结论：股东财富最大化

While profit, growth, and survival are discussed, the uncompromising academic standard in Business Finance establishes that the primary objective is the Maximization of Shareholder Wealth (represented by maximizing the long-term stock price). This is the only metric that effectively captures the magnitude, timing, and risk of future cash flows.

虽然利润、增长和生存都经常被讨论，但在严肃的商业金融学术标准中，企业唯一且首要的目标必须是 股东财富最大化 (Maximization of Shareholder Wealth)（具体表现为最大化公司长期的股票市场价格）。这是唯一能够综合反映未来真实现金流的规模、时间价值和风险属性的科学指标。
