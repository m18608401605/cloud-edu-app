📊 Part 1 — Expected Return & Variance 期望收益与方差

1.1 Expected Return 期望收益率

The expected return of an asset is the probability-weighted average of all possible returns. Think of it as the "fair" average outcome if you could repeat the investment many times.

费曼类比 Feynman Analogy

把期望收益想象成天气预报的加权平均气温：晴天概率30%×25度 + 阴天概率50%×12度 + 下雨概率20%×-5度 = 加权平均气温。期望收益就是这样——不是"最可能的结果"，而是所有可能结果按概率加权后的平均值。

Expected return = probability-weighted average of all possible outcomes. It's the "fair" long-run average if you repeated the investment many times.

Expected Return Formula
E(R) = Σ pᵢ × Rᵢ
pᵢ = probability of scenario i; Rᵢ = return in scenario i
📋 Real Context — UK Equity Market Scenarios

Estimating Expected Return: A UK Equity Fund

A fund manager assigns probabilities to three macro scenarios for the coming year, based on Bank of England forecasts and FTSE 100 historical data:

ScenarioProbabilityReturnp × R
Strong Growth (GDP >2%)0.325%7.5%
Moderate Growth0.512%6.0%
Recession (GDP <0%)0.2−5%−1.0%
E(R)1.012.5%

Note: The FTSE 100 long-run average annual return is approximately 7–10%. A 12.5% expected return reflects an optimistic but plausible scenario mix.

1.2 Variance & Standard Deviation 方差与标准差

费曼类比 Feynman Analogy

把方差想象成射箭的散布程度：期望收益是靶心（平均落点），方差是箭矢落点的分散程度。两个弓箭手平均都射中靶心，但一个每次都很集中（低方差=低风险），另一个忽左忽右（高方差=高风险）。标准差就是把这个散布程度换算回"度数"，让你能直接比较。

Variance measures dispersion around the mean. Standard deviation (σ) is its square root — expressed in the same units as returns (%), making it directly comparable.

Variance Formula
σ² = Σ pᵢ × (Rᵢ − E(R))²
σ = √σ² = Standard Deviation (risk measure)
📝 Continuing — Variance Calculation
ScenarioRᵢ − E(R)(Rᵢ − E(R))²p × (Rᵢ − E(R))²
Strong Growth25% − 12.5% = 12.5%156.2546.875
Moderate Growth12% − 12.5% = −0.5%0.250.125
Recession−5% − 12.5% = −17.5%306.2561.25
σ²108.25 (%²)

σ = √108.25 = 10.4% — this fund has moderate volatility, comparable to a typical actively managed UK equity fund.

🔗 Part 2 — Covariance & Correlation 协方差与相关系数

2.1 Covariance 协方差

费曼类比 Feynman Analogy

把协方差想象成两个朋友的情绪联动：正协方差 = 一起开心、一起难过（股市涨两人都涨）；负协方差 = 一个开心另一个难过（黄金跌股票涨）；零协方差 = 两人情绪完全独立，互不影响。投资组合里，你希望找到"情绪相反"的资产来对冲风险。

Covariance measures how two assets move together. Positive = same direction; Negative = opposite directions; Zero = independent.

Covariance measures how two assets move together. Positive covariance = they tend to move in the same direction. Negative covariance = they tend to move in opposite directions.

Covariance Formula
Cov(A,B) = Σ pᵢ × (Rₐᵢ − E(Rₐ)) × (R_bᵢ − E(R_b))

2.2 Correlation Coefficient 相关系数

Correlation standardises covariance to a range of −1 to +1, making it easier to interpret.

Correlation Formula
ρ(A,B) = Cov(A,B) / (σₐ × σ_b)
ρ = +1: perfect positive | ρ = 0: no relationship | ρ = −1: perfect negative
💡 Key Insight — Diversification

When ρ < +1, combining two assets reduces portfolio risk below the weighted average of individual risks. The lower the correlation, the greater the diversification benefit. When ρ = −1, risk can theoretically be eliminated entirely.

📐 Part 3 — Two-Asset Portfolio Risk 两资产组合风险

3.1 Portfolio Variance Formula

For a portfolio of two assets A and B with weights w_A and w_B (where w_A + w_B = 1):

Two-Asset Portfolio Variance
σ²_p = w²_A σ²_A + w²_B σ²_B + 2·w_A·w_B·Cov(A,B)
Equivalently: σ²_p = w²_A σ²_A + w²_B σ²_B + 2·w_A·w_B·ρ·σₐ·σ_b
📋 Real Context — Diversifying Between Sectors

Portfolio: 60% UK Financials (e.g. Barclays), 40% UK Utilities (e.g. National Grid)

Financials and utilities tend to have low correlation — banks boom in growth cycles, utilities are defensive in downturns. Suppose:

σ_Financials = 10%, σ_Utilities = 15%, ρ = 0.3

Cov = 0.3 × 10% × 15% = 0.45%² (= 45 in %² units)

σ²_p = (0.6)²(100) + (0.4)²(225) + 2(0.6)(0.4)(45)

= 36 + 36 + 21.6 = 93.6 (%²)

σ_p = √93.6 = 9.67%

✅ Weighted average σ would be 0.6×10% + 0.4×15% = 12%. The portfolio σ of 9.67% is 2.33% lower — that's the diversification benefit from ρ = 0.3 < 1.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Students often forget the covariance term (the 2·w_A·w_B·Cov part). Without it, you're just adding variances — which ignores the entire point of diversification.

Always include the 2·w_A·w_B·Cov(A,B) term!
🌐 Part 4 — Efficient Frontier 有效前沿

4.1 The Efficient Frontier Concept

By varying the weights w_A and w_B across all possible combinations (0% to 100%), we trace out a curve in risk-return space. This curve is called the opportunity set or portfolio frontier.

费曼类比 Feynman Analogy — Efficient Frontier

把有效前沿想象成手机的性价比曲线：同样的价格（风险），你想要最好的性能（收益）；同样的性能（收益），你想要最低的价格（风险）。有效前沿上的每一个点都是"性价比最高的组合"——没有任何其他组合能在同等风险下给你更高收益，或在同等收益下给你更低风险。

The efficient frontier = the set of portfolios with maximum return for a given risk level. Rational investors always choose from this set.

📌 Efficient Frontier Definition

The efficient frontier is the upper portion of the portfolio frontier — the set of portfolios that offer the maximum expected return for a given level of risk (or minimum risk for a given return). Rational, risk-averse investors will always choose a portfolio on the efficient frontier.

Key Points:

  • The minimum variance portfolio (MVP) is the leftmost point on the frontier
  • Portfolios below the MVP are inefficient — dominated by portfolios above
  • Adding more assets shifts the frontier further left (more diversification)
  • With many assets, unsystematic risk is diversified away; only systematic risk remains

Systematic vs Unsystematic Risk:

TypeAlso CalledDiversifiable?Examples
SystematicMarket risk, β risk❌ NoInterest rates, recessions, inflation
UnsystematicSpecific, idiosyncratic✅ YesCEO scandal, product recall, strike
📈 Part 5 — CAPM & Beta CAPM与贝塔系数

5.1 The Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)

CAPM provides a precise relationship between an asset's systematic risk (β) and its required return. It is the most widely used model in finance for estimating the cost of equity.

THE CAPM FORMULA

K_e = R_f + β(R_m − R_f)

K_e = Required return on equity  |  R_f = Risk-free rate  |  β = Beta  |  R_m = Market return

(R_m − R_f) = Equity Risk Premium (ERP) — the extra return for bearing market risk

费曼类比 Feynman Analogy — CAPM & Beta

把 Beta 想象成放大镜的倍数：市场涨10%，β=1.5的股票涨15%（放大1.5倍）；市场跌10%，它跌15%。β=0.5的股票只涨5%，跌也只跌5%（缩小到0.5倍）。CAPM 就是说：你承担的"放大倍数"越高，市场要求给你的额外回报也越高。

Beta = sensitivity to market movements. Higher β = more amplification = more systematic risk = higher required return.

5.2 Understanding Beta (β)

Beta measures how sensitive an asset's return is to movements in the overall market. It is the slope of the regression line between the asset's returns and market returns.

β = 0
Risk-Free Asset
No market sensitivity. Return = R_f (e.g., T-bills)
β = 1
Market Portfolio
Moves exactly with the market. Average systematic risk.
β > 1
Aggressive
Amplifies market moves. Higher risk & higher required return.
β < 1
Defensive
Dampens market moves. Lower risk & lower required return.
📋 Real UK Data — Vodafone Group plc

CAPM Applied: What return do investors require from Vodafone?

Using approximate UK market data (as of recent years):

  • R_f = 4.5% (UK 10-year gilt yield)
  • R_m = 10.5% (FTSE 100 long-run expected return)
  • ERP = 10.5% − 4.5% = 6.0%
  • β (Vodafone) ≈ 0.7 (telecoms are defensive — less volatile than the market)

K_e = 4.5% + 0.7 × 6.0% = 4.5% + 4.2% = 8.7%

Interpretation: Vodafone's β < 1 means it's less risky than the average FTSE stock. Investors only require 8.7% — below the market average of 10.5%. Compare this to a high-β stock like a mining company (β ≈ 1.6), which would require: 4.5% + 1.6×6% = 14.1%.

Note: Real betas change over time. These figures are illustrative but based on realistic UK market parameters.

5.3 The Security Market Line (SML)

The SML is the graphical representation of CAPM — a straight line plotting required return against β. All correctly priced assets lie on the SML.

  • Above SML: Asset is underpriced (offers more return than required) → Buy signal
  • Below SML: Asset is overpriced (offers less return than required) → Sell signal
  • Y-intercept: R_f (risk-free rate)
  • Slope: (R_m − R_f) = Equity Risk Premium
⚠️ CAPM Assumptions & Limitations

CAPM assumes: investors are rational and risk-averse; markets are perfect (no taxes, transaction costs); all investors have the same expectations; investors can borrow/lend at R_f.

In practice: β is estimated from historical data and may not predict future risk. CAPM is a model, not reality — but it's the best simple tool we have.
